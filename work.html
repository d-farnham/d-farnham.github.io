Projects & Research

My work spans climate science, data strategy, and systems analysis. Here are some key projects I've led or contributed to significantly, combining rigorous analysis with practical applications.

Climate Risk AI

AI-Driven Climate Forecasting Platform

Project Lead at ClimateAi (2023-Present)

Leading the development of an advanced climate forecasting platform that integrates physical modeling with machine learning to provide actionable climate insights for agriculture, finance, and infrastructure sectors.

Challenge

Traditional climate models lack the resolution and specificity needed for actionable business decisions, while pure ML approaches often lack physical consistency.

Approach

Developed a hybrid approach combining physical climate models with machine learning techniques to create high-resolution, localized climate projections with robust uncertainty quantification.

Impact

Platform now serves over 50 enterprise clients globally, enabling climate-informed decisions that have demonstrably improved supply chain resilience and reduced climate-related losses.

Energy Systems Climate Change

Geophysical Constraints on Renewable Energy Reliability

Research Scientist at Stanford University (2019-2021)

Conducted groundbreaking research on the fundamental geophysical constraints affecting solar and wind power reliability worldwide, with implications for energy system planning under climate change.

Challenge

Understanding the fundamental geophysical limitations of wind and solar resources to inform realistic planning for renewable energy transitions.

Approach

Analyzed global climate data to identify patterns of renewable resource droughts and their relationship to atmospheric circulation patterns and climate change projections.

Impact

Research published in Nature Communications has informed energy policy and planning in multiple countries and contributed to more realistic modeling of 100% renewable energy scenarios.

Climate Risk Energy-Water Nexus

Analysis of the 2021 Texas Cold Snap

Lead Researcher (2021)

Led a rapid-response research initiative to analyze the unprecedented February 2021 Texas cold snap, its relationship to climate change, and implications for infrastructure resilience.

Challenge

Understanding the meteorological drivers and climate context of the extreme cold event that caused catastrophic infrastructure failures across Texas.

Approach

Combined historical climate data analysis with atmospheric dynamics modeling to place the event in historical context and evaluate potential links to Arctic warming.

Impact

Findings published in Environmental Research Letters informed policy discussions about grid resilience and infrastructure weatherization standards.

Water Resources Environmental Engineering

Predictive Modeling for Urban Water Quality

Lead Scientist at Columbia University (2015-2018)

Developed statistical models linking antecedent meteorological conditions to waterway bacterial contamination in urban environments, with applications for public health and regulatory compliance.

Challenge

Creating predictive models for real-time water quality assessment in urban waterways affected by combined sewer overflows and other pollution sources.

Approach

Combined citizen science water quality monitoring with advanced statistical modeling to identify key meteorological predictors of bacterial contamination events.

Impact

Models implemented by local environmental agencies to improve public notifications and recreational water use advisories, enhancing public health protection.
